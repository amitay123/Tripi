# Tripi Design System: The Serene Navigator

## 1. Overview & Creative North Star
The travel experience should never begin with a spreadsheet; it should begin with a breath of fresh air. The Creative North Star for this design system is **"The Serene Navigator."** 

We are moving away from the "utility-only" feel of standard travel apps toward a **High-End Editorial** experience. This means prioritizing "negative space" as a functional element, utilizing intentional asymmetry to guide the eye, and treating typography with the same reverence as a boutique travel magazine. By breaking the rigid, boxed-in grid of traditional apps, we create an interface that feels fluid, organic, and premium.

---

## 2. Colors & Tonal Depth
Our palette is rooted in the clarity of the sky and the softness of a clean slate. We use a sophisticated hierarchy of cool neutrals and deep blues to establish authority without aggression.

### The Palette
- **Primary:** `#0060ad` (The Sky Blue)
- **Primary Container:** `#68abff` (Soft Cloud)
- **Background:** `#f8f9fa` (Crisp Paper)
- **Surface:** `#f8f9fa`
- **Surface Container Low:** `#f1f4f5` (Subtle Depth)
- **Surface Container Lowest:** `#ffffff` (Pure Light)
- **On Surface:** `#2d3335` (Slate Gray - Never Pure Black)
- **Outline Variant:** `#adb3b5` (Ghost Border)

---

## 3. Typography: The Editorial Voice
- **The Display & Headline (Plus Jakarta Sans):** Geometric, modern, and open. Used for destination names and inspirational quotes.
- **The Body & Label (Inter):** High-legibility foundation for complex itineraries.

---

## 4. UI Rules
- **No-Line Rule:** PROHIBITED from using 1px solid borders to define sections. Rely on background color shifts or whitespace.
- **Elevation:** Place a `surface-container-lowest` card on a `surface-container-low` background for a "Zero-G" effect.
- **Corners:** Rounded `xl` (3rem) or `full` for buttons. `md` (1.5rem) or `lg` (2rem) for cards.
- **Negative Space:** Use at least 24px of vertical padding for content groups.
